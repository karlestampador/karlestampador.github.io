Methodology
How It Works

Three-stage pipeline from raw Kaggle data to calibrated win probabilities.

⚙️
Feature Engineering
Built team efficiency metrics from raw box scores, adjusting for pace and opponent quality using Dean Oliver's Four Factors: effective field goal %, turnover rate, offensive rebound rate, and free throw rate. Supplemented with Elo ratings (recency-weighted), Massey Ordinals mean percentile across all ranking systems, and PCA-compressed Massey features for dimensionality reduction.
Four Factors Pace Adjustment Elo Ratings Massey PCA SES Smoothing
🌲
LR + LightGBM Ensemble
Two base models — Logistic Regression and LightGBM — trained on 20+ years of NCAA tournament data. OOF (out-of-fold) predictions from 5-fold stratified cross-validation blended using an ensemble weight. Two-stage hyperparameter optimization: differential evolution searches Elo/stats parameters; Optuna TPE handles LightGBM's mixed integer/float space. Isotonic regression calibration applied on blended OOF scores.
Logistic Regression LightGBM OOF Blending Diff. Evolution Optuna TPE Isotonic Cal.
🎯
Bracket Simulation
A full bracket simulator propagates win probabilities through all 64 tournament slots — handling First Four play-in games and lazy resolution of bracket dependencies. Validated using rolling walk-forward cross-validation on 2010–2025 seasons, ensuring no future data leaks into training. Final model evaluated with Brier score on a 2023–2024 holdout before Kaggle submission.
